Nonna's Lasagna Soup

Some traditions are passed down through many generations, while others are yet to be discovered. My family recipes are always growing, and this one has recently made its way into our collection of delicious heirlooms.

While the soup has quite a few ingredients, it's extremely simple to make, and brings with it a sense of comfort and nostalgia.

Here's What You'll Need

Recipe Ingredients

Make life easier by preparing the below ingredients—both spices and soup—before you're ready to turn up the heat!

Spices

Mix the following six spices in a small bowl and set aside:

  • 1 Tbsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 2 Tbsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 bay leaf

Soup

  • 1 lb of lean ground beef or Italian sausage (sometimes we use both!)
  • 1 medium yellow onion (diced)
  • 4-5 garlic cloves (minced)
  • ¼ tsp red pepper flakes (not mixed with the spices above)
  • 24 oz of your favorite spaghetti sauce (We use Prego or Ragu. Sometimes, I'll make my own from Nonna's book)
  • 8-10 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 14 oz crushed tomato
  • 2 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • Your Italian spices
  • 10 uncooked lasagna noodles, broken into 1-2” pieces
  • ½ pint of half and half or whipping cream
  • Shredded mozzarella, shredded parmesan, or ricotta cheese (topper)

Ready to Start Cooking?

Nonna had a saying about cooking: "any more than 10 steps makin' it, and you're just showin' off." This, of course, did not apply to holiday meals and celebration feasts!

Step-By-Step Instructions

There are only five steps to creating this Italian masterpiece without much fuss.

  1. Brown the beef and onions in a large stock pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Once onions are translucent, add garlic and red pepper, mix well, and drain grease.
  3. Add meat and onions back to stock pot and add spaghetti sauce, six (6) cups of broth, and ALL other ingredients EXCEPT your cream/half and half (you could also cook your noodles separately if you prefer)
  4. Bring to a boil and then lower heat. Simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  5. After 20-30 minutes of simmering, add 2-4 more cups of the broth, and add your cream/half and half. Sometimes, we skip the cream and it's still delicious!

Toppers and Side Items

Top with mozzarella, ricotta, or parmesan cheese (or a bit of all three!).

I usually like to have a fresh, light salad of spring mix, cucumber, red bell pepper, olives, olive oil, and red wine vinegar.

Garlic bread is a traditional side, not to mention delicious.
